The course Design and Analysis of Algorithms helps students understand how problems can be solved efficiently using the best possible methods. It teaches how to design step-by-step logical procedures (algorithms) and how to evaluate their efficiency using mathematical tools.

The course covers algorithmic strategies such as Divide and Conquer, Greedy Method, Dynamic Programming, Backtracking and Graph Algorithms. Students learn how to select the appropriate technique based on the problem.

It also focuses on time and space complexity (Big-O notation), which helps compare algorithms and understand why some solutions are faster or more memory-efficient than others. The course builds strong problem-solving skills and optimization techniques used in modern computing.
